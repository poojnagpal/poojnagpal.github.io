Re-Plate

(2016-2017) My team developed a platform for Replate that supports company sign up/verification, scheduling for food pick-ups, driver task assignment/mapping to destinations, and company payment plans. We helped facilitate the process of matching extra food from businesses or individuals to charities and shelters. Re-Plate is a nonprofit driven to change the way food is conserved and provide ways for businesses and individuals to donate food to those in need. Ruby on Rails/ React JS/ +Frontend

Veterans 360

(current) Designing and developing a mobile application to assist post-veterans. Post 9/11 veterans have high rates of unemployment and struggle to find resources/communities for assistance. Developed Transition Readiness Application, admin web portal, resource vault, and connect dashboard to map and connect veterans to resources. Ruby on Rails/React/Mobile iOS

Women's Safety

((current) Designing and programming an iOS and Android based application for victims in times of danger, with a bracelet that uses Bluetooth to send alerts to emergency contact phones and police within a 5 mile radius. Aimed at solving issue of sexual assault on college campuses. iOS and Android. In progress to work with UC Police department

  • Why?

    • Technical and Nontechnical Projects

    I'm a big believer in the idea that we can best serve society through skills. Thus, it's not a question of whether or not you will dedicate your life to bettering yourself or bettering the lives of others. I see the distinction in how you use the ways you've improved your own skills to best contribute to the lives of others.

    I'm 19. Don't have many skills. But I want to keep growing them. At the age of 15, the greatest skill I had was martial arts, thus attempting to use it the best I could to help girls and women be safe. Now, as I grow older, I hope to use what I think is the most powerful tool in the world, technology, to create a better world for people. I'm still learning.
